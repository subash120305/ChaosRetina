ChaosRetina

Chaos-Based Feature Extraction · Multi-Label Retinal Disease Detection

Standard CNNs smooth over the microscopic differences that separate visually similar retinal diseases. ChaosRetina injects Generalized Luroth Series chaotic maps into the feature pipeline — exploiting sensitive dependence on initial conditions to amplify inter-class differences that conventional deep learning cannot see.

The Clinical Gap

Retinal screening at scale is broken

285 million people live with preventable visual impairment. The bottleneck isn't treatment — it's early detection. Ophthalmologists are scarce; fundus cameras are not.

01 — RARE DISEASE GAP

27 diseases. Most invisible to training.

RFMiD spans 27 disease classes, but rare conditions like Retinitis Pigmentosa appear in under 1% of cases. Standard CNNs trained naively collapse — predicting Diabetic Retinopathy for everything.

Macro-F1 → 0.11 under standard cross-entropy
02 — CNN SMOOTHNESS PROBLEM

Convolution averages out what matters most.

Convolution is linear. Small lesion features cause small activation changes — which vanish in deep networks. The same subtle difference that distinguishes Drusen from Exudates gets averaged out before the classifier ever sees it.

Tiny Δlesion → tiny Δactivation → missed diagnosis
03 — SCALE VS ACCURACY

Bigger backbones don't fix small datasets.

ViT-B/16 models long-range retinal dependencies but requires 85.8M parameters and pre-training on 100K+ images. RFMiD has 3,200. CNNs with inductive biases for local lesion structure are the right prior here.

ViT-B/16 — 16.85 ms/img · 400 MB VRAM
04 — THE CONSTRAINT

Clinical throughput is non-negotiable.

Any viable automated screener must run under 5 ms per image, work on hospital-grade hardware, and produce decisions that can pass clinical audit. Interpretability isn't optional.

EfficientNet-B0 — 1.94 ms/img · 112 MB VRAM

System Design

Two stages. One decision.

A cascaded architecture separates the binary detection problem from the harder multi-label classification — routing only diseased images to the ChaosFEX stage, reducing compute and false alarm rates simultaneously.

01
Stage 1 — Binary Gate

Disease Detector

EfficientNet-B0 acts as a coarse binary classifier: Healthy vs. Diseased. Focal Loss focuses training on the misclassified borderline cases — early-stage presentations that standard binary cross-entropy overlooks. Only diseased images proceed to Stage 2.

Fundus Image [224×224×3] ↓ EfficientNet-B0 (compound-scaled backbone) ↓ Global Average Pooling → [1280-dim vector] ↓ FC(256) → ReLU → Dropout(0.3) → FC(1) ↓ Sigmoid → P(Disease | image) · threshold @ 0.45 → Healthy (stop) ∣ Diseased → Stage 2
AUROC 0.9572 Accuracy 93.8% F1 0.942 Focal Loss γ=2
02
ChaosFEX Module — Core Innovation

Generalized Luroth Series Feature Extraction

CNN feature vectors become initial conditions for 100 chaotic neurons. Each neuron runs 500 GLS map iterations. Four statistics are extracted per neuron — giving a 400-dimensional chaos representation that encodes nonlinear trajectory dynamics invisible to the CNN backbone.

[1280-dim CNN features] ↓ Normalize → (0, 1) ← initial conditions x₀ for 100 neurons ↓ GLS Map: x_{n+1} = (x_n + b·x_n²) mod 1 [b=0.1, 500 iters] ↓ Per-neuron: [MFT, MFR, ME, MEnt] ×100 → [400-dim ChaosFEX] ↓ Linear projection → [1280-dim] ↓ Concat: [1280 CNN ‖ 1280 chaos] = [2560-dim fused]
0 Trainable Params 100 GLS Neurons 500 Iterations Each Butterfly Effect
03
Stage 2 — Multi-Label Classifier

HybridCNNChaosFEX — 27 Disease Classes

The fused 2560-dim representation feeds a classifier head. Each of 27 diseases is treated as an independent binary prediction — sigmoid outputs, BCE loss with per-class positive weights. Per-class threshold optimization independently calibrates rare disease detection.

[2560-dim fused] ↓ FC(512) → ReLU → Dropout(0.4) → FC(27) ↓ Sigmoid → 27 disease probabilities ↓ Per-class threshold: τ* = argmaxτ F1(τ) → Final Multi-Label Prediction
Macro-AUROC 0.8689 Weighted AUROC 0.913 Hamming 94.6% Per-Class Threshold Opt.
04
Interpretability

Iterative Causal Erasure (ICE)

Grad-CAM produces heatmaps — suggestive, but not proof. ICE goes further: slide a window to find the region causing the highest confidence drop, surgically remove it via Telea inpainting, re-evaluate. When the model returns "Healthy," those erased pixels were the causal evidence for the diagnosis.

Original Image → sliding scan: find region → max score drop ↓ Telea Inpainting → digitally healed image ↓ Re-evaluate → still Disease? → loop ↓ Predicts Healthy? → Erased pixels = causal evidence (verified)
Beyond Grad-CAM Causal, Not Correlational Clinical Audit-Ready

The Core Innovation

Why chaos works

The Butterfly Effect isn't metaphor here — it's the mechanism. Sensitive dependence on initial conditions turns a tiny CNN activation difference into a dramatically different chaotic trajectory. The classifier sees what the CNN almost missed.

Generalized Luroth Series Map
xn+1  =  ( xn  +  b · xn2 ) mod 1

b = 0.1  ·  500 iterations per neuron  ·  100 neurons
Initial condition x₀ ∈ (0,1) is the normalized CNN activation. A difference Δx₀ ≈ 10⁻⁴ in the CNN output grows exponentially — after 50+ iterations the trajectories are completely uncorrelated.

MFT
Mean Firing Time

Average gap between threshold crossings. Measures inter-spike regularity of the chaotic trajectory.

MFR
Mean Firing Rate

Threshold crossings per 500 iterations. Higher rate = more oscillatory, active trajectory.

ME
Mean Energy

Mean squared trajectory value. Captures the attractor's energy distribution in phase space.

MEnt
Mean Entropy

Shannon entropy of the trajectory histogram. High entropy = chaotic; low = periodic or fixed-point.

The Butterfly Effect as Feature Extractor

Tiny CNN activation difference Δx₀ → dramatically divergent chaotic trajectory after 50+ GLS iterations → very different [MFT, MFR, ME, MEnt] statistics.

The disease classifier sees this amplified difference, not the original subtle one. Rare disease classes that produce microscopic CNN activation changes become clearly separable in the 400-dimensional chaos feature space.
0
Zero trainable parameters added.
ChaosFEX is a deterministic mathematical transform with no weights and no gradients. The full model stays at EfficientNet-B0's 4.0M parameters while the feature space gains 400 chaotically-derived dimensions.
